# Silent cinema foundations verification

This batch resumes the source-verification workflow for existing Production Cases. It does not add films to the catalogue.

## Verified films

- A Trip to the Moon (1902)
- The Cabinet of Dr. Caligari (1920)
- Nosferatu (1922)
- Battleship Potemkin (1925)

Each film has:

- a source-backed film-history profile;
- a complete 17-area history-and-craft audit;
- a playable comparison against the other silent production systems;
- a verification record with four institutional or archival sources;
- visible separation between `source_verified`, `mapped`, `not_central` and unresolved research.

## Production systems compared

- theatrical trick film, painted tableaux and camera transformation;
- Expressionist studio design, graphic shadows and unreliable narration;
- location-based Weimar horror, physical silhouette and plague imagery;
- Soviet collision montage, collective action and expanded screen time.

## Status handling

Research Control Room derives `verified` from the source registry. A provisional Production Case brief may remain marked `needs_research` until its deeper department mapping is complete, but a registered source audit must still appear as verified in the research queue.

Catalogue totals are tested dynamically so future film additions do not invalidate the verification batch. The verification registry contains 48 source-backed Production Cases after this batch.
