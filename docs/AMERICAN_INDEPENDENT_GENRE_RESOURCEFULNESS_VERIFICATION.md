## Production systems

### Blood Simple

Regional Texas neo-noir built from misunderstanding, restricted locations, lurid colour, object-based suspense, precise ellipsis and sound detail. The profile connects River Road production records, Barry Sonnenfeld cinematography, Jane Musky design, Roderick Jaynes and Don Wiegmann editing, Skip Lievsay sound work, Carter Burwell music and the 1985 Sundance breakthrough.

### Metropolitan

Independently financed New York chamber comedy in which apartments, taxis, formal costume, unknown performers and carefully controlled dialogue replace conventional action. The profile documents the five-week shoot, John Thomas photography, Christopher Tellefsen editing, Mary Jane Fort costume design, monaural sound and festival-to-Oscar reception.

### Reservoir Dogs

Low-budget crime feature that removes the heist, concentrates the action in a mortuary warehouse and reconstructs causality through chapter flashbacks. The profile documents Sundance Lab support, LIVE America and Dog Eat Dog production, Andrzej Sekula's Kodak 5245 colour system, Sally Menke editing, David Wasco design and the fictional K-Billy radio soundtrack.

### El Mariachi

Spanish-language border action feature written around available bars, vehicles, performers and props. The profile documents Los Hooligans production, Robert Rodriguez's combined writing-directing-photography-editing roles, practical effects, economical editorial construction, Sundance circulation, Columbia distribution and National Film Registry recognition.
