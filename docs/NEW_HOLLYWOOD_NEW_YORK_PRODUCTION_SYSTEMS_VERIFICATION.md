# 1970s New York Production Systems — Production Case verification

Verification date: **2026-07-24**

## Scope

This batch verifies four playable scenarios that turn New York into four materially different production systems:

| Film | Year | Production system |
|---|---:|---|
| *Mean Streets* | 1973 | Independently financed neighborhood autobiography built from practical-location economy, volatile performance, Scorsese-controlled editing and structural popular music |
| *Dog Day Afternoon* | 1975 | Real-event institutional procedure built from rehearsal, confined geography, changing light, crowd and media feedback and virtually scoreless sound |
| *Taxi Driver* | 1976 | Subjective post-Vietnam neo-noir built from diary voice-over, expressionist location photography, slow motion, Herrmann music and altered climax color |
| *Manhattan* | 1979 | Romanticized city construction built from black-and-white Panavision, Kodak Double-X, selective lighting, static widescreen blocking, Gershwin and optical effects |

## Verification standard

Every film now has:

- all 17 Film Study coverage areas
- at least 9 `source_verified` areas
- 4 inspectable HTTPS sources
- at least 2 distinct source publishers
- a source-backed Production Case registry record
- dedicated resolver and comparison-choice tests

## Source hierarchy

The batch prioritizes:

1. **AFI Catalog** for production history, credits, release context and recorded production decisions.
2. **British Film Institute / Sight and Sound** for film-historical position, dramaturgy, performance, music and formal analysis.
3. **American Society of Cinematographers** for first-hand technical accounts from Victor J. Kemper, Michael Chapman and Gordon Willis.
