Verified on 2026-07-23. This batch reviews existing playable scenarios only; it adds no films.

## Comparative production systems

### Happening

Annie Ernaux's autobiographical history is reconstructed through one student's narrowing field of choices in 1963 France. A 1.37 frame, shallow subjective attention, week markers, ordinary student rooms, bodily sound and increasingly sustained procedure make illegality operate through time, secrecy and physical risk.

### All the Beauty and the Bloodshed

Nan Goldin's photographs, recorded testimony, family and queer-community history, P.A.I.N. actions and museum responses form one double narrative. Chaptered montage, slideshows, protest footage, meetings and institutional records make biography itself evidence for collective action against Sackler philanthropy and the opioid crisis.

### Dahomey

Twenty-six royal treasures move from a Paris museum through packing, transport, arrival, installation and debate in Benin. Precise digital observation, crate and gallery sound, ceremony, student discussion and the transformed first-person voice of object 26 make restitution simultaneously material procedure, historical imagination and unresolved politics.

### Nickel Boys

Historical fiction is rebuilt around the embodied viewpoints of Elwood and Turner. Sony VENICE modular builds, Panavision VA lenses, body and helmet rigs, remote heads, shallow focus, 360-degree sets, archival fragments and associative editing make gaze, touch and withheld sight the film's ethical structure.

## Verification threshold

Each case now has:

- four inspectable HTTPS sources from at least two publishers;
- a complete 17-area history and craft audit;
- at least nine `source_verified` areas;
- explicit `mapped` or `not_central` status where department-level evidence is incomplete;
- one playable history match, one partial match and one mismatch;
- a verification record linked to the existing scenario ID.

## Sources

The batch uses festival archives, national and international film institutes, official production pages and detailed craft records from the European Film Academy, La Biennale di Venezia, British Film Institute, Unifrance, Film at Lincoln Center, Participant, Busan International Film Festival, Berlin International Film Festival, British Board of Film Classification, Film Independent, Sony Cine and Panavision.

Sources support only the registry's permitted areas: `overall`, `screenplay`, `cinematography`, `editing` and `sound`.

## Integration

The four profiles are exposed through the existing player-facing independent-storytelling resolver and use a dedicated donor pool. Alternatives compare embodied historical procedure, activist archive montage, restitution as institutional and speculative process and radical sentient first-person cinematography rather than unrelated contemporary films.
