# Silent and early studio systems verification

This quality-control batch verifies four existing Production Cases. It does not add films to the catalogue.

## Verified films

- The Phantom Carriage (1921)
- The General (1926)
- Metropolis (1927)
- Frankenstein (1931)

## Production systems compared

- Swedish literary silent cinema built through nested flashbacks, Filmstaden staging and layered double exposure;
- American independent silent comedy built through full-body performance, real locomotives, practical stunt engineering and spatially legible editing;
- UFA science-fiction spectacle built through architecture, miniatures, optical combination, mass choreography and orchestral silent-film presentation;
- Universal sound horror built through stage adaptation, studio design, makeup, creature performance, electrical props, mobile photography and recorded sound.

Each film has:

- a source-backed history profile;
- a complete 17-area history-and-craft audit;
- a playable comparison against the other three production systems;
- five inspectable institutional or archival sources;
- explicit separation between `source_verified`, `mapped`, `not_central` and unresolved research.

## Evidence base

The batch uses records from the Swedish Film Institute, British Film Institute, AFI, Museum of Modern Art, Library of Congress, filmportal.de, Deutsche Kinemathek, UNESCO and Criterion.

The verification registry contains 52 source-backed Production Cases after this batch. Catalogue size remains dynamic and is not duplicated in this document.
