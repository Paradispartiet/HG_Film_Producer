## Comparative production systems

### Secrets & Lies

Mike Leigh develops characters separately with the actors through research and rehearsal before fixing the scenes for filming. Dick Pope's composed images, portrait sessions, domestic rooms, long exchanges and the birthday-barbecue ensemble turn adoption, race, class and loneliness into lived relationships rather than explanatory themes.

### Dancer in the Dark

Handheld factory melodrama collides with Björk's musical imagination. Naturalistic photography, harsh social spaces and distressed editing give way to songs assembled from machine rhythms, choreography and expanded multi-camera coverage, allowing musical vision to grow as Selma's sight disappears.

### The Son's Room

A close family is established through running, meals, work, jokes, music and ordinary movement before sudden death breaks those repetitions. Restrained performance, clear domestic geography, psychoanalytic listening, measured editing and small changes in rooms and journeys make grief a continuing alteration of everyday time.

### Winter Sleep

A former actor-landlord, his wife, his sister and indebted tenants are enclosed in a Cappadocian cave hotel for 196 minutes. Snowbound landscape, deep interiors, fire, windows, prolonged confrontations and withheld reactions make intelligence and language themselves instruments of property, marital control and self-deception.

## Verification threshold

Each case now has:

- four inspectable HTTPS sources from at least two publishers;
- a complete 17-area history and craft audit;
- at least nine `source_verified` areas;
- explicit `mapped` or `not_central` status where department-level evidence is incomplete;
- one playable history match, one partial match and one mismatch;
- a verification record linked to the existing scenario ID.

## Sources

The batch uses festival archives, national film institutes, restoration catalogues, museum and cinematheque records, production documentation and professional craft records from Festival de Cannes, British Film Institute, The Criterion Collection, Danish Film Institute, Filmitalia, Museum of Modern Art, La Cinémathèque française, European Film Academy and Gökhan Tiryaki.

Sources support only the registry's permitted areas: `overall`, `screenplay`, `cinematography`, `editing` and `sound`.

## Integration

The four profiles are exposed through the existing player-facing independent-storytelling resolver and use a dedicated donor pool. Alternatives compare devised family performance, social realism interrupted by musical fantasy, routine-centred bereavement and long-duration verbal class power rather than unrelated European dramas.
