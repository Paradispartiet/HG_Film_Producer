# Chinese-language modernity and memory Production Case verification

Verified on 2026-07-23. This batch reviews existing playable scenarios only; it adds no films.

## Comparative production systems

### Taipei Story

A contemporary relationship drama turns offices, apartments, traffic, windows, television and emigration plans into evidence of a rapidly modernising Taipei. Restrained performance, exact 35mm compositions, daylight, negative space, ellipsis and Tu Duu-chih's urban sound field make private incompatibility inseparable from economic and architectural change.

### A City of Sadness

The 1945-49 transition from Japanese rule to Nationalist government is dispersed through one family rather than explained by a single historical protagonist. Fixed long takes, deep domestic space, letters, several languages, radio, offscreen violence and large temporal gaps make incomplete witnessing the form of political memory.

### Farewell My Concubine

A Peking opera partnership crosses childhood training, war, occupation, revolution and the Cultural Revolution. Repeated roles, bodily discipline, costume, makeup, colour, crowds, applause, slogans and editorial echoes make performance both intimate identity and public historical evidence.

### Platform

A provincial cultural troupe moves from 1979 state organisation toward 1990 private enterprise and imported popular culture. Long 35mm views, real Fenyang environments, ensemble staging, travel, announcements, songs and decade-scale ellipses make reform history accumulate through changing work and ordinary distance.

## Verification threshold

Each case now has:

- four inspectable HTTPS sources from at least two publishers;
- a complete 17-area history and craft audit;
- at least nine `source_verified` areas;
- explicit `mapped` or `not_central` status where department-level evidence is incomplete;
- one playable history match, one partial match and one mismatch;
- a verification record linked to the existing scenario ID.

## Sources

The batch uses official film records, restoration catalogues, festival archives, film-institute programmes and production reporting from Taiwan Cinema, Taiwan Film and Audiovisual Institute, The Film Foundation World Cinema Project, Film at Lincoln Center, British Film Institute, La Biennale di Venezia, The Criterion Collection, Hong Kong Film Archive, Festival de Cannes, Toronto International Film Festival programme archives and The New Yorker.

Sources support only the registry's permitted areas: `overall`, `screenplay`, `cinematography`, `editing` and `sound`.

## Integration

The four profiles are exposed through the existing player-facing independent-storytelling resolver and use a dedicated donor pool. Alternatives compare contemporary urban alienation, family-centred political memory, opera performance across regime change and provincial reform-era duration rather than unrelated Chinese-language films.
