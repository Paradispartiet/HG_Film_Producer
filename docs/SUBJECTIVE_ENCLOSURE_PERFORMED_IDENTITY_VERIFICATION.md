# Subjective enclosure and performed identity Production Case verification

Verified on 2026-07-23. This batch reviews existing playable scenarios only; it adds no films.

- `scenario_burning_2018`

## Comparative production systems

### Burning

A Murakami adaptation is restricted to an insecure aspiring writer whose observations are socially precise but never fully reliable. Patient digital widescreen, peripheral action, dusk, glass, rural borders, delayed repetition, jazz and charged silence turn class resentment, masculine projection and a woman's disappearance into one unresolved system.

### An Elephant Sitting Still

Four intersecting lives unfold across one oppressive day and nearly four hours. Intimate tracking shots, shallow focus, obstructed foregrounds, limited reverse angles, industrial-town sound and delayed intersections keep the social world physically close but visually inaccessible.

### Kagemusha

After years of funding difficulty, Kurosawa converts extensive preparatory paintings into a large Toho-Fox historical epic supported internationally by George Lucas and Francis Ford Coppola. A thief's performance as a dead warlord is sustained through ceremony, colour-coded armies, armour, landscape, weather, silence and mass choreography.

### Barton Fink

A playwright enters the 1941 studio system and becomes trapped in a decaying hotel and a wrestling-film assignment. Roger Deakins's first Coen collaboration, Dennis Gassner's production design, repeating corridors, peeling wallpaper, heat, low room noise, long conversation and abrupt genre rupture turn creative labour into a subjective chamber nightmare.

## Verification threshold

Each case now has:

- four inspectable HTTPS sources from at least two publishers;
- a complete 17-area history and craft audit;
- at least nine `source_verified` areas;
- explicit `mapped` or `not_central` status where department-level evidence is incomplete;
- one playable history match, one partial match and one mismatch;
- a verification record linked to the existing scenario ID.

## Sources

The batch uses official festival archives, national film databases, cinematography trade records, restoration catalogues and repertory criticism from Festival de Cannes, Korean Film Council, British Film Institute, Film at Lincoln Center, Film Comment, The Criterion Collection, AFI Catalog and American Society of Cinematographers.

Sources support only the registry's permitted areas: `overall`, `screenplay`, `cinematography`, `editing` and `sound`.

## Integration

The four profiles are exposed through the existing player-facing independent-storytelling resolver and use a dedicated donor pool. Alternatives compare restricted class viewpoint, four-hour durational enclosure, political impersonation at epic scale and creative labour inside a surreal studio-hotel system.
