# Scandinavian and European Production Case expansion

## Purpose

The shared Film Producer catalogue is broad, but Scandinavian and European film history remains thinner than the American and recently expanded Asian coverage. This round adds a curated bridge between early European film language, postwar modernism, Nordic national traditions and contemporary European production practice.

The expansion is additive and identity-safe. If a target film already exists in the 315-case catalogue, the existing case is reused only when title, year and original-title or director identity agree.

## Production-case coverage

Each definition supplies film-specific targets for:

- screenplay structure and dramatic pressure
- cinematography, staging and location logic
- editing rhythm and information control
- sound, music, silence and environmental texture
- a concrete learning objective tied to the film's production method

All newly created entries remain marked `manual_scandinavian_european_case_needs_source_verification` and resolve to production briefs with `verificationStatus: "needs_research"`.

## Integrity rules

The test suite requires:

- exactly 22 unique target definitions
- each target to resolve exactly once after merging
- no duplicate scenario IDs in the final catalogue
- same-title and same-year synthetic films with unrelated directors to be rejected
- catalogue growth to equal the number of genuinely missing targets rather than an assumed fixed number

This is the first regional balancing round. Later European batches can deepen Eastern Europe, Iberia, the Balkans, the Baltic states, Ireland, the United Kingdom and contemporary documentary and animation without rebuilding this foundation.
