# South Korean historical, social and genre-hybrid Production Case verification

Verified on 2026-07-23. This batch reviews existing playable scenarios only; it adds no films.

## Cases

- `scenario_peppermint_candy_1999`
- `scenario_oasis_2002`
- `scenario_the_host_2006`
- `scenario_the_wailing_2016`

## Comparative production systems

### Peppermint Candy

Seven chapters move backward from 1999 to 1979. Repeated railway geography and reverse-moving train transitions connect one damaged life to military repression, Gwangju-era violence, labour and economic transformation.

### Oasis

A socially abrasive romance uses Uijeongbu locations, physically exact performance, patient observation and brief subjective transformations without allowing fantasy to erase disability, family neglect or unequal power.

### The Host

A digitally created creature appears in daylight across recognizable Han River public space. Family rescue, political satire, comedy, horror and a divided Korean-American VFX pipeline operate as one popular genre system.

### The Wailing

An extended rural investigation combines natural-light rain photography, detailed spatial planning, family infection, special makeup, competing rituals and long postproduction so every urgent interpretation remains potentially catastrophic.

## Verification threshold

Each case now has:

- at least four inspectable HTTPS sources from at least two publishers;
- a complete 17-area history and craft audit;
- at least nine `source_verified` areas;
- explicit `mapped` or `not_central` status where department-level evidence is incomplete;
- one playable history match, one partial match and one mismatch;
- a verification record linked to the existing scenario ID.

## Sources

The batch uses records and interviews from the Korean Film Council, Museum of Modern Art, La Biennale di Venezia Historical Archive, British Film Institute and Festival de Cannes. Sources support only the registry's permitted areas: `overall`, `screenplay`, `cinematography`, `editing` and `sound`.

## Integration

The profiles are exposed through the already integrated film-history profile resolver. South Korean profiles use a dedicated four-film donor pool, so their historical alternatives compare reverse chronology, social realism, family monster hybridity and occult procedural uncertainty rather than unrelated systems.
