## Shared production question

The films form a comparison group around American independent cinema's use of regional production to examine identity, migration, family, community and belonging:

- institutional impersonation, race and unreliable self-narration in *Chameleon Street*
- diasporic history, interracial romance and motel economies in *Mississippi Masala*
- three female viewpoints, family labour and desert-town fantasy in *Gas Food Lodging*
- childhood guilt, community memory and lyrical Southern landscape in *George Washington*

The group is registered as:

`american_regional_identity_place_belonging`

## Chameleon Street

Primary records:

- AFI Catalog
- British Film Institute
- Sundance Institute festival history
- Wendell B. Harris Jr. filmmaker interview

Verified elements include Prismatic Images, Flint-area financing and production, the long screenplay-development process, Harris's writer-director-editor-star role, Dan Noga cinematography, Tim Alvaro production design, first-person narration, role-based costume and speech, the 1990 Sundance Grand Jury Prize, distribution exclusion and later restoration.

## Mississippi Masala

Primary records:

- The Criterion Collection
- AFI Catalog
- Sundance Institute

## Gas Food Lodging

## George Washington
