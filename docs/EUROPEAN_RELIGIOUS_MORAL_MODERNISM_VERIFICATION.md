## Scope

This quality-lift batch closes every remaining unverified Film Producer scenario from the 1960s:

- *Viridiana* (1961)
- *The Gospel According to St. Matthew* (1964)
- *Au Hasard Balthazar* (1966)

The films are grouped because each turns religious or moral institutions into a concrete production system rather than treating belief as theme alone.

## Distinct systems

### Viridiana

Buñuel's return-to-Spain production uses an inherited estate, devotional objects, controlled black-and-white tableaux, restrained principal performance and the beggars' banquet to expose the relationship among charity, property, desire and anti-clerical satire.

### The Gospel According to St. Matthew

Pasolini replaces prestige biblical spectacle with southern Italian locations, nonprofessional faces, rough black-and-white observation, direct scripture, historical design and a soundtrack crossing Bach, Mozart and spiritual traditions.

### Au Hasard Balthazar

Bresson builds ethical and spiritual pressure through model performance, animal presence, fragments of hands and objects, elliptical editing, offscreen action, bells, hooves, machinery and selective Schubert.
